# YouTube Video Analysis Prompt

Analyze this YouTube video transcript thoroughly. Extract maximum value.

## 1. Overview
- **Title & Creator**: Who made this and what's the topic?
- **Content Type**: Tutorial, interview, essay, news, review, etc.
- **Target Audience**: Who is this for?
- **Core Thesis**: What's the main argument or message in 1-2 sentences?

## 2. Comprehensive Summary
Provide a thorough 3-4 paragraph summary covering:
- The problem or context being addressed
- The main arguments or solutions presented
- Key examples or evidence used
- Conclusions and implications

## 3. Key Takeaways (All Important Points)
List ALL significant points, not just top 5-7. Be exhaustive:
- Main arguments and claims
- Supporting points and sub-arguments
- Conclusions and recommendations
For each, be specific - include numbers, names, specifics mentioned.

## 4. Facts, Statistics & Data
Extract every specific fact mentioned:
- Numbers, percentages, dates
- Research findings cited
- Statistics and metrics
- Comparisons and benchmarks

## 5. Frameworks, Models & Concepts
Capture any mental models or frameworks introduced:
- Named frameworks (e.g., "80/20 rule", "Jobs to be Done")
- New terminology or definitions
- Categorizations or taxonomies
- Decision-making frameworks

## 6. Tools, Resources & References
List everything mentioned:
- Software, apps, products
- Books, papers, articles referenced
- People and organizations mentioned
- Websites, courses, other resources

## 7. Examples & Case Studies
Document specific examples used:
- Real-world case studies
- Hypothetical scenarios
- Before/after comparisons
- Success/failure stories

## 8. Notable Quotes
Include 5-10 memorable quotes with approximate timestamps if visible.
Prioritize quotes that:
- Capture key insights
- Are memorable/quotable
- Represent controversial or unique views

## 9. Actionable Insights
What can you DO with this information?
- Immediate actions (today/this week)
- Short-term projects (this month)
- Long-term strategies
- Things to research further

## 10. Questions & Gaps
- What questions does this raise?
- What wasn't addressed?
- What needs more research?
- Potential counterarguments?

## 11. Latent Signals
Surface insights that are implied but not explicitly stated. Only include genuine inferences - do NOT fabricate signals.
- **Unstated assumptions**: What does the speaker take for granted?
- **Implied predictions**: What future trends or outcomes are suggested?
- **Hidden motivations**: Why might they be saying this now?
- **Second-order effects**: What downstream consequences follow from these ideas?
- **Market/industry signals**: What does this suggest about where things are heading?
- **Contrarian indicators**: What's conspicuously NOT being said?
- **Emotional subtext**: What concerns, fears, or excitements underlie the content?

## 12. Connections
- Related topics to explore
- Similar content/creators
- Prerequisite knowledge helpful
- Follow-up content suggestions

---
Format the output as a clean markdown report.
