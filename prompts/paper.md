# Research Paper Analysis Prompt

Analyze this research paper thoroughly. Make it accessible while preserving depth.

## 1. Paper Overview
- **Title**: Full paper title
- **Authors & Affiliations**: Who wrote it, from where
- **Publication**: Journal/conference, year
- **Paper Type**: Empirical, theoretical, survey, position paper, etc.

## 2. Plain English Summary
What did they do and why does it matter? 3-4 paragraphs covering:
- The problem they're solving
- Their approach and what's new about it
- What they found
- Why anyone should care

## 3. Research Question & Motivation
- What specific question are they answering?
- Why is this question important?
- What gap in knowledge are they filling?
- What's the practical motivation?

## 4. Key Contributions
List ALL contributions claimed:
- Novel methods or algorithms
- New datasets or benchmarks
- Theoretical insights
- Empirical findings
- Practical applications

## 5. Methodology Deep Dive
- **Data**: What data did they use? Size, source, characteristics
- **Methods**: What techniques did they apply?
- **Experiments**: What experiments did they run?
- **Baselines**: What did they compare against?
- **Evaluation Metrics**: How did they measure success?

## 6. Results & Findings
Extract ALL specific results:
- Performance numbers (accuracy, F1, etc.)
- Comparisons to baselines (X% improvement)
- Statistical significance
- Ablation study results
- Qualitative findings

## 7. Limitations & Caveats
- Stated limitations
- Unstated limitations you notice
- Threats to validity
- What we can't conclude
- Edge cases not addressed

## 8. Technical Details
For readers wanting depth:
- Key equations or algorithms
- Architecture details
- Hyperparameters
- Implementation specifics
- Computational requirements

## 9. Related Work Context
- How does this fit with prior work?
- Key papers they build on
- Competing approaches
- What's different about their approach

## 10. Practical Implications
- Real-world applications
- Who would use this and how
- Implementation considerations
- Industry relevance

## 11. Future Directions
- What do the authors suggest next?
- Open problems identified
- Extensions you could imagine
- Research gaps remaining

## 12. Critical Assessment
- Strengths of the work
- Weaknesses or concerns
- Reproducibility considerations
- Claims that seem overclaimed

## 13. Latent Signals
Surface insights that are implied but not explicitly stated. Only include genuine inferences - do NOT fabricate signals.
- **Unstated assumptions**: What theoretical or practical assumptions underlie this work?
- **Implied directions**: What future research directions are implicitly suggested?
- **Field dynamics**: What does this paper suggest about where the field is heading?
- **Second-order effects**: If this work succeeds, what downstream effects follow?
- **Competitive signals**: What does the framing suggest about competing approaches?
- **Reading between the lines**: What concerns or limitations are downplayed?
- **Timing significance**: Why now? What does the timing of this work suggest?

## 14. References to Follow
Key papers to read for:
- Background understanding
- Competing approaches
- Follow-up work
- Deeper technical details
