# Article Analysis Prompt

Analyze this article thoroughly. Extract maximum value and insights.

## 1. Metadata
- **Title & Author**: Who wrote this?
- **Publication/Source**: Where was it published?
- **Article Type**: Opinion, research summary, how-to, news, essay, etc.
- **Core Thesis**: Main argument in 1-2 sentences

## 2. Comprehensive Summary
Provide 3-4 paragraphs covering:
- The problem or context being addressed
- The author's main argument and supporting logic
- Key evidence and examples used
- Conclusions and call to action

## 3. All Key Points
Extract EVERY significant argument and claim:
- Primary arguments
- Supporting sub-points
- Evidence for each claim
- Counterarguments addressed
Be specific - include numbers, names, specifics.

## 4. Facts, Statistics & Data
Extract all specific data mentioned:
- Numbers, percentages, dates, timelines
- Research studies cited (author, year, finding)
- Statistics and metrics
- Comparisons and benchmarks
- Sources for the data

## 5. Frameworks & Mental Models
Capture conceptual tools introduced:
- Named frameworks or models
- New terminology defined
- Categorizations or typologies
- Decision-making heuristics

## 6. Examples & Evidence
Document all examples used:
- Case studies with specifics
- Anecdotes and stories
- Historical examples
- Hypothetical scenarios

## 7. People, Companies & References
- Individuals mentioned (who they are, why relevant)
- Companies and organizations
- Books, papers, studies cited
- Other articles or resources linked

## 8. Author's Perspective & Bias
- Author's background and credentials
- Potential biases or conflicts
- Unstated assumptions
- What the author might be wrong about

## 9. Notable Quotes
5-10 memorable passages that:
- Capture key insights
- Are well-articulated
- Represent unique perspectives

## 10. Actionable Takeaways
What to DO with this information:
- Immediate applications
- Changes to consider
- Things to research further
- Decisions this informs

## 11. Critical Analysis
- Strengths of the argument
- Weaknesses or gaps
- Counterarguments to consider
- What's missing from the analysis

## 12. Latent Signals
Surface insights that are implied but not explicitly stated. Only include genuine inferences - do NOT fabricate signals.
- **Unstated assumptions**: What does the author take for granted?
- **Implied predictions**: What future trends or outcomes are suggested?
- **Hidden motivations**: Why is this being published now? What agenda might exist?
- **Second-order effects**: What downstream consequences follow from these ideas?
- **Market/industry signals**: What does this suggest about where things are heading?
- **Contrarian indicators**: What's conspicuously NOT being addressed?
- **Reading between the lines**: What can be inferred from tone, emphasis, or omissions?

## 13. Connections
- Related reading
- Topics to explore further
- Prerequisites for understanding
- Contrasting viewpoints to seek

---
Format the output as a clean markdown report.
