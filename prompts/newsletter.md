# Email/Newsletter Analysis Prompt

You are analyzing an email newsletter or forwarded email content. Your goal is to extract key insights, summarize the content, and identify actionable items while filtering out promotional noise.

## Analysis Structure

### 1. Newsletter Overview
Provide essential context:
- **Newsletter/Sender name** and publication frequency
- **Edition/Issue** - date or issue number
- **Primary topic(s)** covered in this edition
- **Content type** - curated links, original essays, industry news, personal updates, etc.
- **Estimated reading time** of original

### 2. Executive Summary
Summarize the newsletter in 2-3 paragraphs:
- Main theme or focus of this issue
- Most important points covered
- Key takeaways for the reader
- Why this issue matters

### 3. Key Insights & Ideas
Extract the most valuable content:
- **Main arguments or claims** made
- **Novel perspectives** or contrarian takes
- **Trends identified** or predictions made
- **Frameworks or mental models** introduced
- **Industry insights** or market observations

### 4. Curated Links & Resources
If the newsletter contains curated content:
- List linked articles/resources with brief descriptions
- Categorize by topic or type
- Highlight which links seem most valuable
- Note any paywalled or limited-access content

### 5. Data & Statistics
Capture any quantitative information:
- Statistics or metrics cited
- Research findings mentioned
- Market data or trends
- Benchmarks or comparisons

### 6. Notable Quotes
Extract memorable passages:
- Quotable insights from the author
- Quotes from sources they cite
- Particularly well-articulated ideas

### 7. Recommendations & Picks
Document any recommendations made:
- **Tools/Products** - software, services, hardware
- **Books/Reading** - books, articles, papers suggested
- **People/Follows** - accounts, creators to follow
- **Events** - conferences, webinars, meetups
- **Other resources** - courses, podcasts, videos

### 8. Action Items
Identify actionable takeaways:
- Things to try or experiment with
- Habits or practices to adopt
- Resources to explore further
- People to follow or connect with
- Ideas to implement

### 9. Author's Perspective
Understand the writer's viewpoint:
- Author's background and expertise
- Potential biases or blind spots
- Recurring themes in their writing
- What they seem bullish/bearish on

### 10. Relevance Assessment
Evaluate the content's value:
- **Most valuable for** - who benefits most from this content
- **Skip if** - when this newsletter isn't relevant
- **Signal vs noise ratio** - how much is genuinely useful
- **Subscription worthy?** - is ongoing subscription worthwhile

### 11. Latent Signals
Read between the lines:
- Unstated assumptions
- Implied predictions
- Hidden product/service pitches
- Subtle positioning or agenda
- What's conspicuously absent

### 12. Follow-up Items
Track things to revisit:
- Topics to research further
- Links to read later
- Questions raised to explore
- Experiments to run
- Ideas to revisit in future

### 13. Summary
Conclude with:
- **One-line summary** - the newsletter in a sentence
- **Key takeaway** - single most important insight
- **Worth reading in full?** - or is summary sufficient
- **Archive or delete?** - is this worth keeping

---

## Guidelines

- Distinguish between original content and curated links
- Note sponsored content or affiliate links transparently
- Filter out promotional fluff to focus on substance
- Preserve specific recommendations with context
- For recurring newsletters, note how this issue compares
- Extract ALL links and resources mentioned
- Be skeptical of hype and buzzwords
