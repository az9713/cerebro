# Content Analysis Prompt

Analyze this content thoroughly. Extract maximum value.

## 1. Overview
- **Content Type**: What kind of content is this?
- **Source/Author**: Who created it?
- **Core Topic**: What is this primarily about?
- **Main Message**: What's the key point in 1-2 sentences?

## 2. Comprehensive Summary
3-4 paragraphs covering:
- Context and background
- Main ideas and arguments
- Supporting evidence or examples
- Conclusions or implications

## 3. All Key Points
List EVERY significant idea:
- Main arguments
- Supporting points
- Evidence presented
- Conclusions drawn
Be specific with numbers, names, details.

## 4. Facts & Specifics
Extract all concrete information:
- Numbers, dates, statistics
- Names and references
- Specific examples
- Verifiable claims

## 5. Concepts & Frameworks
- New terms or definitions
- Mental models introduced
- Categorizations or frameworks
- Key distinctions made

## 6. Examples & Illustrations
- Case studies used
- Analogies and metaphors
- Real-world applications
- Hypothetical scenarios

## 7. Resources & References
- Tools or products mentioned
- Books, articles, sources cited
- People or organizations referenced
- Links or resources shared

## 8. Notable Passages
5-10 memorable quotes or sections worth highlighting.

## 9. Actionable Insights
- What can you do with this?
- Decisions this informs
- Things to research further
- Changes to consider

## 10. Questions Raised
- What's left unanswered?
- What needs more exploration?
- Potential counterarguments?
- Gaps in the content?

## 11. Latent Signals
Surface insights that are implied but not explicitly stated. Only include genuine inferences - do NOT fabricate signals.
- **Unstated assumptions**: What does the creator take for granted?
- **Implied predictions**: What future trends or outcomes are suggested?
- **Hidden motivations**: Why was this created? What purpose beyond the obvious?
- **Second-order effects**: What downstream consequences follow from these ideas?
- **Reading between the lines**: What can be inferred from tone, emphasis, or omissions?
- **Contrarian indicators**: What's conspicuously NOT being said?

## 12. Connections
- Related topics to explore
- Prerequisites for understanding
- Complementary content
- Contrasting viewpoints

---
Format the output as a clean markdown report.
