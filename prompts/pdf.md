# PDF Document Analysis Prompt

Analyze this PDF document thoroughly. Extract maximum value and insights regardless of document type (report, whitepaper, ebook, manual, etc.).

## 1. Document Overview
- **Title**: Document title
- **Author(s)/Organization**: Who created this?
- **Document Type**: Report, whitepaper, ebook, manual, presentation, research, etc.
- **Date**: When was it created/published?
- **Purpose**: What is this document meant to accomplish?
- **Target Audience**: Who is this written for?
- **Core Message**: Main thesis or purpose in 1-2 sentences

## 2. Comprehensive Summary
Provide 3-4 paragraphs covering:
- The context and background
- Main arguments, findings, or content
- Key evidence and supporting material
- Conclusions and recommendations

## 3. All Key Points
Extract EVERY significant point, finding, or argument:
- Primary claims and assertions
- Supporting evidence for each
- Data and statistics
- Conclusions drawn
Be exhaustive - capture all valuable information.

## 4. Facts, Statistics & Data
Extract all specific data mentioned:
- Numbers, percentages, metrics
- Dates, timelines, milestones
- Research findings and citations
- Comparisons and benchmarks
- Projections and forecasts
- Tables, charts, graphs (describe the data)

## 5. Frameworks, Models & Concepts
Capture conceptual tools and structures:
- Named frameworks or methodologies
- Key terminology and definitions
- Process flows and workflows
- Categorizations or typologies
- Decision trees or matrices

## 6. Visual Content Analysis
If the document contains visuals:
- Diagrams and their meaning
- Charts and what they show
- Infographics and key takeaways
- Screenshots or examples

## 7. Examples & Case Studies
Document all examples used:
- Real-world case studies
- Implementation examples
- Before/after scenarios
- Success stories or cautionary tales

## 8. People, Organizations & References
- Individuals mentioned and their relevance
- Companies and organizations
- Products, tools, or technologies
- Books, papers, websites cited
- External resources referenced

## 9. Structure & Organization
How the document is organized:
- Major sections and their purposes
- Logical flow of arguments
- Appendices and supplementary material
- Index or glossary terms

## 10. Notable Quotes
5-10 memorable passages that:
- Capture key insights
- Summarize important concepts
- Represent unique perspectives
- Are particularly well-articulated

## 11. Actionable Takeaways
What to DO with this information:
- Immediate applications
- Implementation steps
- Decisions this informs
- Things to research further
- Changes to consider

## 12. Critical Analysis
- Strengths of the document
- Weaknesses or gaps
- What's missing
- Potential biases
- Accuracy and reliability assessment

## 13. Latent Signals
Surface insights that are implied but not explicitly stated. Only include genuine inferences - do NOT fabricate signals.
- **Unstated assumptions**: What does the author take for granted?
- **Implied predictions**: What future trends or outcomes are suggested?
- **Hidden motivations**: Why was this document created? What agenda might exist?
- **Second-order effects**: What downstream consequences follow from this content?
- **Industry/market signals**: What does this suggest about where things are heading?
- **Contrarian indicators**: What's conspicuously NOT being addressed?
- **Reading between the lines**: What can be inferred from emphasis, tone, or omissions?

## 14. Connections
- Related documents to seek
- Topics to explore further
- Prerequisites for deeper understanding
- Complementary resources

---
Format the output as a clean markdown report.
