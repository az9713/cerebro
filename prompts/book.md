# Book/EPUB Analysis Prompt

You are analyzing a book or book chapter. Your goal is to provide deep, comprehensive analysis that helps the reader extract maximum value and remember key insights long after reading.

## Analysis Structure

### 1. Book/Chapter Overview
Provide essential context:
- **Title and Author(s)** with brief author background if notable
- **Publication context** - when written, what prompted it, target audience
- **Genre/Category** - academic, self-help, biography, technical, fiction, etc.
- **Scope** - what this book/chapter covers and doesn't cover
- **Reading time estimate** and difficulty level

### 2. Executive Summary
A comprehensive summary in 4-5 paragraphs:
- The central thesis or main argument
- How the author builds their case
- Key supporting evidence or stories
- Major conclusions reached
- Why this matters to the reader

### 3. Core Thesis & Arguments
Extract the fundamental ideas:
- **Main thesis** - the single most important claim
- **Supporting arguments** - 3-7 major points that support the thesis
- **Evidence types used** - research, anecdotes, logic, case studies
- **Logical structure** - how arguments build on each other

### 4. Key Concepts & Frameworks
Document important mental models:
- New terminology or concepts introduced
- Frameworks or models presented (with diagrams if text-based)
- Theories or principles explained
- Distinctions and categorizations made

### 5. Memorable Examples & Stories
Capture the illustrative content:
- Significant case studies or examples
- Personal anecdotes from the author
- Historical references
- Thought experiments or hypotheticals
- Why each example matters

### 6. Data, Statistics & Research
Document empirical content:
- Key statistics and numbers cited
- Research studies referenced
- Data sources mentioned
- Quantitative claims made

### 7. Notable Quotes
Extract the most powerful passages:
- Quotable insights (with page/location if available)
- Memorable phrases or formulations
- Passages that capture core ideas succinctly
- Controversial or thought-provoking statements

### 8. Practical Applications
Identify actionable takeaways:
- Specific advice or recommendations
- Step-by-step processes described
- Tools, techniques, or methods
- Exercises or practices suggested
- How to apply concepts in real life

### 9. Critical Analysis
Provide objective evaluation:
- **Strengths** - what the book does well
- **Weaknesses** - gaps, oversimplifications, or flaws
- **Assumptions** - underlying beliefs the author holds
- **Biases** - potential blind spots or one-sidedness
- **What's missing** - perspectives or counterarguments not addressed

### 10. Connections & Context
Situate the work in broader context:
- How it relates to other books on the topic
- Intellectual influences on the author
- Schools of thought it aligns with or challenges
- Contemporary debates it engages with
- How ideas have aged (for older books)

### 11. Reader Relevance Assessment
Help readers calibrate expectations:
- **Best for** - who will benefit most
- **Skip if** - when this book isn't the right choice
- **Prerequisites** - knowledge helpful before reading
- **Pairs well with** - complementary reading suggestions

### 12. Latent Signals
Infer what's between the lines:
- Author's unstated motivations
- Implied predictions about the future
- Hidden assumptions about human nature
- What the author seems uncertain about
- Tensions or contradictions in the argument

### 13. Personal Application Prompts
Questions for the reader to reflect on:
- 5-7 questions to deepen engagement with the material
- Prompts for applying ideas to one's own life
- Exercises to test understanding
- Discussion questions for book clubs

### 14. Summary & Verdict
Conclude with:
- **One-sentence summary** - the book in a nutshell
- **Key takeaway** - the single most important insight
- **Rating/Recommendation** - who should read this and why
- **Read the full book?** - is the summary sufficient or is full reading essential?

---

## Guidelines

- Quote the author directly when capturing key insights
- Include chapter references or page numbers when possible
- Distinguish between author's claims and your analysis
- Note when ideas are controversial or contested
- Be comprehensive - extract ALL valuable content, not just highlights
- For fiction: focus on themes, character insights, and literary merit
- For chapter analysis: relate to the book's overall arc
