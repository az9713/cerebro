# Twitter/X Thread Analysis Prompt

Analyze this Twitter/X thread thoroughly. Extract maximum value from this conversational, bite-sized content format.

## 1. Thread Overview
- **Author**: Who wrote this thread? (handle, name, credentials)
- **Thread Topic**: What is this about?
- **Tweet Count**: How many tweets in the thread?
- **Core Thesis**: Main argument in 1-2 sentences
- **Thread Type**: Educational, opinion, story, how-to, announcement, etc.

## 2. Comprehensive Summary
Provide 2-3 paragraphs covering:
- The main point being made
- How the argument develops across tweets
- Key evidence or examples used
- Final conclusions or calls to action

## 3. All Key Points
Extract EVERY significant claim and insight:
- Main arguments (one per tweet often)
- Supporting points
- Data or evidence cited
- Recommendations made
Number these to show the thread's progression.

## 4. Facts, Statistics & Data
Extract all specific data mentioned:
- Numbers, percentages, metrics
- Dates and timelines
- Research or studies cited
- Comparisons and benchmarks
- Links shared and their purpose

## 5. Frameworks & Mental Models
Capture conceptual tools introduced:
- Named frameworks or models
- New terminology or concepts
- Lists or categorizations
- Decision-making heuristics

## 6. Examples & Stories
Document all examples used:
- Personal anecdotes
- Case studies mentioned
- Before/after scenarios
- Screenshots or images described

## 7. People & Resources Mentioned
- Other Twitter users tagged or quoted
- Companies and products
- Books, articles, or tools recommended
- Links and resources shared

## 8. Author Analysis
- What is the author's background?
- What unique perspective do they bring?
- What biases might they have?
- What's their likely motivation for this thread?

## 9. Notable Quotes
5-10 individual tweets or phrases that:
- Capture key insights concisely
- Are particularly quotable
- Represent contrarian or unique views

## 10. Actionable Takeaways
What to DO with this information:
- Immediate actions to take
- Ideas to implement
- Things to research further
- People to follow or resources to check

## 11. Critical Analysis
- Strengths of the thread
- What's missing or oversimplified
- Counterarguments to consider
- What would strengthen the argument

## 12. Latent Signals
Surface insights that are implied but not explicitly stated. Only include genuine inferences - do NOT fabricate signals.
- **Unstated assumptions**: What does the author take for granted?
- **Implied predictions**: What future trends are suggested?
- **Hidden motivations**: Why share this now? What agenda?
- **Audience signals**: What does thread popularity suggest?
- **Platform dynamics**: How does Twitter format affect the message?
- **Contrarian indicators**: What's NOT being said?

## 13. Connections
- Related threads or content to find
- Topics to explore further
- Contrasting viewpoints to seek
- Authors to follow for similar content

---
Format the output as a clean markdown report.
